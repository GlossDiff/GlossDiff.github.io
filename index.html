Abstract

Cued Speech (CS) is an advanced visual phonetic1 encoding system that integrates lip reading with2 hand codings, enabling people with hearing im-3 pairments to communicate efficiently. CS video4 generation aims to produce specific lip and gesture's movements of CS from audio or text inputs. The6 main challenge is that given limited CS data, we7 strive to simultaneously generate fine-grained hand8 and finger movements, as well as lip movements,9 meanwhile the two kinds of movements need to10 be asynchronously aligned. Existing CS genera-11 tion methods are fragile and prone to poor per-12 formance due to template-based statistical models13 and careful hand-crafted pre-processing to fit the14 models. Therefore, we propose a novel Gloss-15 prompted Diffusion-based CS Gesture generation16 framework (called GlossDiff). Specifically, to in-17 tegrate additional linguistic rules knowledge into18 the model. we first introduce a bridging instruc-19 tion called Gloss, which is an automatically gen-20 erated descriptive text to establish a direct and21 more delicate semantic connection between spo-22 ken language and CS gestures. Moreover, we23 first suggest rhythm is an important paralinguis-24 tic feature for CS to improve the communication25 efficacy. Therefore, we propose a novel Audio-26 driven Rhythmic Module (ARM) to learn rhythm27 that matches audio speech. Moreover, in this work,28 we design, record, and publish the first Chinese29 CS dataset with four CS cuers. Extensive experi-30 ments demonstrate that our method quantitatively31 and qualitatively outperforms current state-of-the-32 art (SOTA) methods.
